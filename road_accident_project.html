Introduction

Road safety is a critical concern, and understanding the factors contributing to road accidents is essential for informed decision-making. This Tableau dashboard presents a comprehensive analysis of road accidents in the Kensington and Chelsea district for the year 2019. The dashboard is designed to provide insights into accident severity, casualties, weather conditions, road conditions, vehicle types, and more. Users can interact with the dashboard by selecting the year of interest, allowing for dynamic exploration of accident data.


Dasboard Explaination

The dashboard comprises several key components: Number of Casualties for Current Year and Previous Year: Users can select the year of interest using a parameter. This section provides an overview of the total number of casualties for the selected year and the previous year, enabling users to compare trends. KPIs (Total Accidents, Total Casualties, Serious Casualties, Fatal Casualties, Slight Casualties): A set of key performance indicators summarizes critical accident statistics. Users can quickly assess the total number of accidents and different casualty categories. Types of Vehicles and Casualties: A data table or chart visualizes the relationship between vehicle types and casualties. It helps identify which types of vehicles are involved in accidents and their associated casualties. Donut Charts for Weather Conditions and Road Conditions: Two donut charts illustrate the distribution of accidents based on weather conditions and road conditions. Users can gain insights into how different conditions impact accident severity. Horizontal Bar Chart for Casualties by Road Surface: A horizontal bar chart presents casualties based on road surface conditions. It allows for easy comparison of casualty rates on various road surfaces. Map for Casualties by Location: A map provides a geographical view of accident locations. Users can explore casualty data by zooming in and clicking on specific locations.



Results

The analysis of road accidents in Kensington and Chelsea for 2019 yielded the following key findings: The total number of accidents increased compared to the previous year. Serious casualties were the most common type, followed by slight casualties. Accidents primarily occurred in urban areas and on single carriageways. Daylight conditions were more prevalent during accidents. The road surface condition significantly impacted the severity of accidents, with wet or damp conditions leading to more casualties. This dashboard equips stakeholders with valuable insights to devise strategies for improving road safety, such as targeted interventions in high-risk areas or during adverse weather conditions.
