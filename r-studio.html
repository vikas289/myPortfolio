Introduction

The analysis focuses on examining the progression of average hourly wages (AWR) against the Consumer Price Index (CPI) in Canada and Ontario over the last four years. Utilizing data sourced from governmental statistics, the aim is to elucidate the relationship between wages and inflation to assess the financial well-being of residents in these regions.


Code Explaination

The analytical process begins by cleaning and organizing the wage and inflation data for Canada and Ontario, respectively. A year-over-year comparison is conducted to decipher the percentage changes in AWR and CPI. The insights derived from this comparison are visualized through graphical representations. The generated graphs portray the trends in AWR and CPI from March 2019 to March 2023. They demonstrate consistent growth in AWR over time in both Canada and Ontario, except for a slight decline in 2020 attributed to the pandemic. Conversely, the inflation rates have remained relatively stable throughout the four-year period, with a marginal uptick observed in 2022.



Results

The analysis indicates that residents in Ontario have experienced a financial trajectory analogous to the broader trends observed in Canada. The upward movement in AWR suggests economic growth, signifying employers' ability to offer higher wages, fostering a robust job market in both regions. Additionally, the steadiness in inflation implies a controlled rise in living costs, positively impacting residents' purchasing power. However, it's essential to note that these insights encapsulate a partial view of financial well-being. Variables like income disparity, job security, and affordable housing accessibility remain unaccounted for in this analysis. Certain segments of Ontario's populace might not have witnessed equivalent financial progress, underscoring the need for a comprehensive understanding of individual financial circumstances beyond these metrics. In conclusion, while the data indicates a parallel financial trajectory between Ontario and Canada in terms of wage growth and inflation stability, it's crucial to consider a broader spectrum of socioeconomic factors to ascertain a comprehensive depiction of residents' financial situations.
